Keynote speakers

Sylvia Xueni Pan

Professor of Virtual Reality Department of Computing


Virtual Social Interaction: Applications for Health

Biography: Prof Sylvia Pan is a Professor of Virtual Reality at Goldsmiths, University of London. She co-leads the SeeVR research lab including 10 academics and researchers. She holds a PhD in Virtual Reality, and an MSc in Computer Graphics, both from UCL, and a BEng in Computer Science from Beihang University, Beijing, China. Her research interest is the use of Virtual Reality as a medium for real-time social interaction, in particular in the application areas of medical training and therapy. Her work in social anxiety in VR and moral decisions in VR has been featured multiple times in the media, including BBC Horizon, the New Scientist magazine, and the Wall Street Journal. Her 2017 Coursera VR specialisation attracted over 100,000 learners globally, and she co-leads on the MA/MSc in Virtual and Augmented Reality at Goldsmiths Computing.

Mónica S. Cameirão


Customized interactive technologies for health and wellbeing

Healthcare is growingly incorporating new assistance delivery models that rely on the use of gaming and virtual reality technologies. Virtual reality has a lot of potential because it allows the creation of paradigms that explore the role of personalization of training, the use of various types of feedback, and engagement by incorporating tasks in game contexts. In addition, through these tools we can monitor patients and have a precise and objective quantification of their performance and recovery over time. In this presentation, I will describe different paradigms that combine virtual reality, games, neurosciences, and clinical guidelines with the objective of validating interactive systems specifically designed for applications for health and wellbeing. I will particularly focus on aspects such as the personalization of tasks and content. The impact of these paradigms will be illustrated with the results from controlled studies with end-users.

Biography: Mónica is an Assistant Professor at the department of Informatics Engineering and Interactive Media Design at the University of Madeira (UMa). She is the current Director of the PhD Program in Informatics Engineering. Mónica is a co-founder of the NeuroRehabLab Research Group, an interdisciplinary research group that investigates at the intersection of technology, neuroscience, and clinical practice to find novel solutions to increase the quality of life of those with special needs. Mónica has been particularly involved in the development and clinical assessment of Virtual Reality (VR) technologies for stroke rehabilitation, exploring specific brain mechanisms that relate to functional recovery to approach motor and cognitive stroke rehabilitation by means of non-invasive and low-cost technologies. Her research addresses aspects such as serious gaming and personalization of training. More recently, Mónica has also been interested in VR for mental health applications. In 2016, Mónica has been awarded the ISVR Early Career Investigator Award, an award granted by the International Society for Virtual Rehabilitation to recognize and acknowledge outstanding contributions by early career scientists whose research relates to virtual rehabilitation. From 2020, Mónica is a member of the Ethics Committee of UMa.

Marko Suvajdzic


AI/Blockchain/XR - The Merging of Emerging Technologies in Healthcare

AI, Blockchain, and VR/AR are the three emerging technologies that are poised to define the future, each on its own, and in the case of medicine by fusing into the next generation of digital solutions to create better quality healthcare systems. At the University of Florida, we are working closely with NVIDIA to create the initial prototype of an intelligent hospital. By creating a digital twin of a hospital, we are accomplishing three incredible technological achievements. The hospital digital twin can be controlled through VR, all interactions and access points are controlled through smart contracts on Blockchain, and all interactions are run through an AI engine to determine the best responses. It is through the combination of AI, Blockchain, and VR, that the field of healthcare is about to experience the next step in its evolution.

Biography: Marko Suvajdzic is a diverse thinker with 20+ years of achievement in academia and the creative digital research and production space. Marko’s experience includes projects ranging from artificial Intelligence-intensive video game titles for major industry publishers, VR applications for health, to co-founding five of his own startups. Marko is a researcher and the Associate director of Digital Worlds Institute, and the director oif Blockchain Reearch Lab at the University of Florida. Marko has lectured internationally at schools and conferences in U.S.A., U.K., India, UAE, Serbia, China, Australia, Norway, and Japan.
